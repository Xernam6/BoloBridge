The Vocal Cords

The strings that give voice to air

Your vocal cords are like two tiny rubber bands stretched across your throat. When air from your lungs pushes through them, they vibrate to create sound — just like plucking a guitar string!

The faster they vibrate, the higher your pitch. When you whisper, they open wide to let air pass silently. When you sing, they dance in perfect harmony to shape the notes of your unique voice.

Try This!

Hum gently and touch your throat. Can you feel the vibrations? Those are your vocal cords at work!
