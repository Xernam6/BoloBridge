0:00 Imagine a delicate instrument tucked away inside your throat. These are your vocal folds, often called vocal cords. They aren't strings in the literal sense, but two flexible bands of muscle tissue.

0:45 When you prepare to speak, these bands draw together. As air from your lungs pushes past them, they begin to oscillate—vibrating back and forth with incredible precision. This is what creates the initial sound wave.

1:24 The pitch you hear is determined by how fast these folds vibrate. Higher notes require more tension, much like tightening a guitar string. A child's vocal cords might vibrate much faster than an adult's, leading to a higher natural frequency.
